# Mokosh Threads

**A digital archive, marketplace, and storytelling project inspired by Slavic heritage, mythology, craftsmanship, and horology.**

Mokosh Threads is a Singapore-based independent creative project exploring the intersection of **Slavic culture, traditional clothing, vintage Soviet and Russian watches, mythology, historical objects, and digital storytelling**.

The website is designed not simply as an online shop, but as an immersive digital world where every garment, watch, card, and relic carries a story.

## About the Project

Mokosh Threads began with an interest in traditional Slavic clothing and has since evolved into a broader cultural and creative platform.

The collection includes:

* Traditional Slavic clothing and embroidered garments
* Vintage Soviet and Russian mechanical watches
* Vostok watch dials and horological curiosities
* Vintage Soviet cameras and historical objects
* Handcrafted Slavic jewellery and accessories
* Mokosh Cards, a collection of 175 illustrated Slavic mythological figures
* Singapore mythology and legends cards
* Original stickers and creative merchandise

At its heart, Mokosh Threads is about discovering the stories hidden within objects—whether that means the embroidery of a traditional dress, the mechanical movement of a Soviet watch, or a forgotten creature from ancient folklore.

## The Website

The Mokosh Threads website is a custom-built static website hosted using GitHub Pages.

It was designed to combine a lightweight static architecture with a cinematic, atmospheric visual experience.

The website features:

* Responsive layouts for desktop and mobile
* A cinematic scroll-based homepage inspired by Slavic mythology
* Parallax effects and layered environmental animations
* Dynamic product galleries
* JSON-driven watch and dial collections
* Interactive product modals
* Filtering and sorting systems
* Sold-item status and archival functionality
* Telegram and WhatsApp enquiry integration
* Multi-currency price display
* Dedicated collections for clothing, watches, relics, cards, stickers, and watch dials

## Collections

### Traditional Clothing

A curated collection of traditional and Slavic-inspired clothing, including embroidered dresses, shirts, folk garments, and handcrafted pieces.

Each item is presented not simply as clothing, but as part of a living cultural tradition.

### Vintage Soviet & Russian Watches

Mokosh Threads features a growing collection of vintage watches from the Soviet Union and Russia, including pieces from manufacturers such as:

* Vostok
* Poljot
* Raketa
* Slava
* Luch
* Molnija
* Pobeda
* Chaika
* ZIM

The collection places particular emphasis on Vostok watches, including the legendary Amphibia, Komandirskie, Generalskie, commemorative editions, military-themed dials, and unusual historical variants.

### Vostok Dial Archive

The website also contains a dedicated digital archive of Vostok watch dials, documenting different designs, rarities, manufacturing periods, conditions, origins, and dial codes.

The archive is intended both as a visual collection and as a growing reference for fellow Soviet watch enthusiasts.

### Mokosh Cards

Mokosh Cards is an original collection of **175 illustrated characters from Slavic mythology**, encompassing gods, heroes, spirits, supernatural beings, monsters, and mythical animals.

The collection is divided into five thematic series:

**Thrones of the Old Gods**
35 deities and divine beings.

**Oaths of Crown and Sword**
41 nobles, heroes, and heroines.

**Whispers of Hearth & Forest**
36 household, nature, and seasonal spirits.

**Veils of Sorcery**
30 shapeshifters, supernatural beings, and undead figures.

**Fangs of Myth & Oath**
33 monsters, magical allies, and mythical animals.

Together, the five collections form a visual journey through the strange, beautiful, and often forgotten world of Slavic folklore.

## Design Philosophy

The visual identity of Mokosh Threads draws inspiration from:

* Ancient Slavic forests
* Folklore and mythology
* Traditional embroidery
* Soviet industrial design
* Vintage horology
* Archival photography
* Cinematic dark fantasy
* Museum exhibitions and cabinets of curiosities

The homepage is designed as a journey through an enchanted forest, where visitors gradually encounter different shrines representing the major collections of Mokosh Threads.

The goal is to make browsing feel less like navigating a conventional e-commerce website and more like discovering objects and stories within a forgotten archive.

## Technology

The website is built primarily with:

* HTML5
* CSS3
* JavaScript
* JSON
* GitHub Pages

The project intentionally uses a static architecture to remain lightweight, fast, transparent, and easy to maintain without requiring a traditional backend or content management system.

Dynamic collections are managed through structured JSON data, allowing new watches, dials, products, and archive entries to be added without rebuilding the underlying interface.

## AI-Assisted Creative Process

Mokosh Threads explores the use of generative AI as part of its creative and development workflow.

AI-assisted tools have been used in areas including:

* Concept development
* Visual experimentation
* Mythological illustration
* Website development
* Coding assistance
* Animation concepts
* Historical research
* Creative writing and storytelling

AI is treated as a creative tool rather than a substitute for the historical objects, physical products, traditional garments, vintage watches, and cultural sources that form the foundation of the project.

## Project Status

Mokosh Threads is an actively evolving personal project.

New collections, watches, historical objects, mythology cards, visual experiments, and interactive features will continue to be added over time.

The website itself is also an ongoing experiment in combining static web development with cinematic storytelling, generative AI, historical collecting, and independent e-commerce.

## About the Creator

Mokosh Threads was created by **Wan Ariff**, a Singapore-based creator, collector, and developer with interests spanning artificial intelligence, web development, Slavic culture, mythology, vintage Soviet and Russian watches, digital storytelling, and historical objects.

Having lived and studied in Russia for several years, the project grew from a personal fascination with the objects, traditions, stories, and histories encountered along the way.

Mokosh Threads brings these interests together into one evolving digital archive—part collection, part marketplace, and part creative experiment.

## Disclaimer
