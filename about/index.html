Our Story

Heritage, craftsmanship, and stories carried across borders.

Mokosh Threads is a Singapore-based husband-and-wife business dedicated to curating authentic Slavic traditional clothing, vintage Soviet watches, mechanical film cameras, and cultural objects with meaningful stories.

Our collection began through personal travel, friendships with craftspeople, and a shared appreciation for handmade traditions. We work with independent makers, small workshops, collectors, and trusted contacts to bring carefully selected pieces to Singapore.

From Russia to Singapore

The Story of Mokosh Threads

A journey shaped by travel, craftsmanship, friendship, and a growing appreciation for objects that carry history.

May 2023
Gorodets / craft workshop photograph

Gorodets, Russia

The City of Masters

The story began when Nastya and Wan travelled to Gorodets, known as the City of Masters. There, they were introduced to local craftspeople and discovered workshops preserving traditional Slavic decorative arts, handmade garments, and regional craft techniques.

The visit left a lasting impression and introduced them to the people behind these cultural objects.

June 2023
Suzdal Cucumber Festival photograph

Suzdal, Russia

A Meeting with Daria

During a visit to Suzdal for the annual Cucumber Festival, Nastya and Wan met a travelling craftswoman named Daria, who was selling premium traditional costumes.

Her clothing stood out for its quality, detail, and connection to traditional design. This encounter helped shape their appreciation for authentic Slavic garments produced by independent makers.

2024
Russia to Singapore travel photograph

A New Chapter

Moving to Singapore

Nastya and Wan moved to Singapore for work. Although they were now far from Russia, their interest in Slavic crafts and cultural history continued.

They remained in contact with craftspeople and small workshops across Russia and Ukraine.

November 2025
Garments, sourcing, or preparation photograph

The Beginning of the Brand

The Idea for Mokosh Threads

Wan discovered the upcoming Renfaire festival in Singapore and saw an opportunity to introduce authentic Slavic medieval-inspired clothing to a new audience.

Together, Nastya and Wan began contacting trusted makers and craftspeople in Russia and Ukraine, sourcing garments from small workshops similar to those they had first encountered during their travels.

31 January 2026
First Mokosh Threads booth photograph

The First Booth

Mokosh Threads Meets Singapore

The garments arrived just in time for the festival. On 31 January 2026, Nastya and Wan opened the first Mokosh Threads booth.

Alongside the traditional clothing, Wan displayed part of his personal collection of Soviet mechanical watches and vintage film cameras. Visitors responded warmly, and the watches and cameras quickly became an unexpected highlight of the event.

2026 & Beyond
Current Mokosh Threads collection photograph

A Growing Collection

Vintage Finds. Timeless Stories.

Following the festival, Mokosh Threads expanded beyond clothing to include vintage Soviet watches, film cameras, crafts, and other carefully selected objects.

The brand has since participated in additional events and continues to sell through Carousell and online. The collection will continue to grow slowly and thoughtfully, guided by authenticity, craftsmanship, and stories worth preserving.

Our Approach

Curated, Not Mass Produced

Mokosh Threads is not a mass-market retailer. Many of the items we offer come from small workshops, individual makers, vintage collections, and trusted contacts.

We prioritise authenticity, craftsmanship, cultural context, and objects with stories behind them.

01

Authenticity

Pieces selected with care, context, and respect for their origins.

02

Craftsmanship

04

Slow Curation
